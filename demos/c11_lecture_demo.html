Basemap Development with Mapbox & ArcGIS Online

Part I

Basemap Development - Parameters

For interactive web mapping, the basemap typically operates as the ‘base’ layer that provides contextual information to the end user. Basemap layers are typically a Server-side application that is responsive to Client-side actions:

Basemaps serve as a reference map on which you overlay data from layers and visualize geographic information. An individual basemap can be made of multiple feature, raster, or web layers. Basemaps are the foundation for your [interactive, online] maps and provide context for your work.

A basemap service - often referred to as a tiled service - contains cached tiles that render the map in small tile images. This allows the map to perform much faster when panning and zooming. Most contemporary basemap services follow a typical web address structure.

To start, navigate to openwhatevermap.xyz/ and take a look at a collection of out-of-the-box basemap options. Click on some of the gridded basemap options and notice the tile address structure:

https://stamen-tiles-{s}.a.ssl.fastly.net/toner-lite/{z}/{x}/{y}{r}.png
  • The first part of the web address specifies the origination of the tile service. The second {z}/{x}/{y}{r} structure allows for zoom and positioning provided by the user through the browser. The final .png refers to the image type for the tile service.

Tile Services typically fall into one of two categories:

  • Raster map tiles are larger in size, less demanding on end-users’ hardware but more demanding on the server-side performance.

  • Vector map tiles are faster to load, and less demanding on the server-side performance but more demanding on end-users hardware.

Often there is also a required API Access Token attached to both raster and vector tile services that have been customized by a particular user within the user’s account be it ArcGIS Online or Mapbox.
