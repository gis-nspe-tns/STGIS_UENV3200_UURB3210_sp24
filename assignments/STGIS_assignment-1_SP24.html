Preamble

In this first assignment, you will utilize the Class 1 Technical Lab as a guide to complete your first map utilizing the Natural Earth dataset.

The Class 1 Technical Lab should be used extensively to aid with the completion of the first assignment due Sunday evening (01/28/24) at 11:59pm.

Unlike the lab, you are tasked with choosing an unique approach to this first map. Your map should feature consideration of the following:

  • Map Scale: do you want to create a global map (small scale), or a regional map (‘larger’ scale) - corridor, region, state, continent ?
  • Map Theme(s): do you want to create a simple map theme or one that is more complex, i.e. more than 1 or 2 vector layers?
  • Page/paper orientation and size: will your final output be landscape or portrait orientation? Either letter or tabloid, landscape or portrait is acceptable for this first mapping assignment.

Assignment Step 1

  • Navigate to the directory folder Natural_Earth_quick_start then double-click the Natural_Earth_quick_start_for_ArcMap file. Conversely, utilize the ArcCatalog to connect to the Natural_Earth_quick_start directory and add in your thematic vector layers one-by-one into the Table of Contents.

Assignment Step 2

  • Determine your mapping theme(s) and begin to consider your map scale. Utilize Spatial Bookmarks in your .mxd project at the Data Frame to establish your location, scale, theme(s) that you will refine in Layout View. Keep in mind, cartographic design can go wrong with either too few or too many thematic layers; map legibility is the goal.

Assignment Step 3

  • Use Map Symbology for your thematic layer(s) to create balance and map legibility.

Assignment Step 4

  • Finalize your map in Layout View and incorporate a title and legend into the final design. Also include a small tag in your map notating yourself as the author of the map.

  • Output your final map as letter or tabloid, landscape or portrait orientation, as PDF or PNG. Both file types will be accepted at the CANVAS assignment submission location.

  • Make sure to continuously save your assignment .mxd and maintain good file management within your project directory.

Concluding Remarks

Utilize the example maps below for the required map elements - map title, author notation, legend and the map itself. Note that the example maps feature just 2 thematic layers - the Natural Earth countries feature and Natural Earth populated places. Your mapping doesn’t necessarily have to be limited to just to themes.

Upload your assignment.1.pdf to the CANVAS location for the first class assignment. Save the project directory with all project materials in a location you’ve set aside for completed assignments for the course.
